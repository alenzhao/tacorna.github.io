TACO: Transcriptome meta-assembly for RNA-Seq

Transcriptome assemblers reconstruct full-length transcripts from the short sequence fragments generated by RNA-Seq. Large consortia such as TCGA, GTex, ENCODE, the Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia (CCLE), and many others have performed RNA-Seq on thousands of human tissues and cell lines, providing an unparalleled resource for investigating transcriptional diversity and complexity. Towards this end, we present Transcriptome Assemblies Combined into One (TACO), an algorithm that reconstructs a consensus transcriptome from a large collection of individual assemblies. By employing rigorous change point detection and a novel dynamic programming approach TACO vastly outperforms meta-assembly software tools such as Cuffmerge and Stringtie merge. See the results of our comparison analysis here.
